🧠 Why This Works
Languages have rules about how sounds can combine:

English: Loves consonant clusters → "strengths" (CCCCCC)

Turkish: Hates vowel sequences → almost no VV pairs

Latin/Romance: Balanced, clean alternating patterns

These rules are baked into the history and evolution of each language:

Germanic languages → consonant clusters

Turkic languages → vowel harmony (no adjacent vowels)

Romance languages → open syllables (CV preference)

Your statistics are capturing centuries of linguistic evolution in four numbers! 🤯
